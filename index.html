Bridging the Financial Gap

Millions of micro-entrepreneurs rely on informal lending because traditional banks can't reach them. The institutions that do serve them still run on paper, pen, and manual labor. That creates real problems.

Disaster Vulnerability

When a typhoon hits, physical records are gone for good. Passbooks, ledgers, and client files stored in filing cabinets have zero protection against natural disasters.

Information Blackouts

Provincial branches operate in isolation. Head office has no way to see what's happening in real time, creating blind spots that can lead to errors, delays, or even fraud.

Administrative Overload

Computing employee incentives, daily collections, and borrower balances by hand takes weeks every cycle. Staff spend more time on paperwork than actual service.

What SumTrack Can Do

Built for real-world microfinance. Every feature solves a specific pain point.

Automated Risk Assessment

Field collectors write daily notes about each borrower. SumTrack processes those notes through Natural Language Processing and automatically flags accounts as Okay, Warning, or Risky.

Automated Payroll

Employee incentives are calculated automatically based on collection performance. No more manual spreadsheets, no bias, no delays.

Borrower Digital Passbook

Clients can view their loan history, payment schedules, and remaining balances anytime through a simple, dedicated portal.

Mobile Field Access

Field collectors use their own phones to check daily targets and performance rankings on the go, without needing access to sensitive data.

Real-Time Sync

All branch transactions are synced to the cloud in real time, giving head office a live view of cash flow, collections, and disbursements.

Secure Data Storage

All data is encrypted and backed up daily, ensuring that no information is ever lost, even in the event of a disaster.

Deployment Partner

Sum Finance Services Corp.

Established in 1996 and headquartered in Tagbilaran City, Bohol, Sum Finance is a microfinance institution that provides capital to micro-entrepreneurs like sari-sari store owners, carinderia vendors, and small traders who don't have access to traditional banks.

Over nearly three decades, the institution has grown to 32 satellite branches and over 250 personnel nationwide. But the systems behind it all are still paper-based: physical passbooks, handwritten index cards, and typewritten ledgers. SumTrack is built to change that.
